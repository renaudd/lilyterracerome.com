Chronicles of Regola

A Legacy written in Stone

The Rione Regola is one of Rome's most enduring neighborhoods, a district where the ancient craft of the Renaissance persists in the shadow of imperial history. It is here, nestled along the Vicolo del Giglio, that Lily Terrace marks its place—a residence born from a lineage of artistic and historic significance.

In the late 20th century, this terrace served as the residence for Ramón Gaya, the celebrated Spanish painter and writer. Gaya's presence imbued the space with a quiet, intellectual dignity that we have meticulously preserved. Voted "the most beautiful city in the world" by the very artists who called it home, Regola remains the last bastion of authentic Roman nobility.

"Fleur-de-lis means lily flower in French; Giglio means lily in Italian. Vicolo del Giglio (Lily Lane) derives its name from the crest of the House Farnese—a field of fleur-de-lis—whose historic palace stands just steps away in Piazza Farnese."

The House Farnese

The proximity to Palazzo Farnese, now the French Embassy, defines the local architectural character. The influence of the Farnese family brought with it a standard of elegance that is reflected in the surrounding piazzas and narrow lanes. To walk the stones of Vicolo del Giglio is to follow the path of popes, ambassadors, and masters of the High Renaissance.
